Getting Started

Learn the basics of WaypointMap interface and workflow.

1. Select Your Drone

Before planning, go to the Settings Tab on the left sidebar and select your drone model (e.g., DJI Mavic 3E). This ensures all calculations for camera sensor width and focal length are accurate.

2. Choose Units

Toggle between Metric (m) and Imperial (ft) in the settings panel. This will update all distance and speed inputs instantly.

Drawing Tools

Polygon Tool

Use this to define a survey area. Click multiple points to form a closed shape. Double-click to finish.

  • Click to add vertex
  • Double-click to close polygon

Waypoint Tool

Manually drop single waypoints for simple missions or to adjust a path.

Tip: After drawing, you can drag the white handles to adjust vertices, or use the ghost handles (transparent ones) to add new points.

Photogrammetry Auto-Calc

This is the core feature for surveyors. It automatically generates a "lawn-mower" flight path to cover your polygon.

  1. Draw a Polygon over your target area.
  2. Set your desired Altitude (AGL).
  3. Input optimal Side Overlap and Front Overlap (e.g., 70% / 80%).
  4. Click Apply to Settings. The system will calculate the required Line Spacing and Shooting Interval.
  5. Finally, click Generate Waypoints to visualize the path.

Exporting to DJI Pilot 2

Once you are satisfied with your mission:

  1. Go to the Export Tab.
  2. Check the estimated Total Distance and Time.
  3. Enter a Mission Name.
  4. Click Download KMZ.
Import Instructions
Copy the .kmz file to your remote controller's storage:
/DJI/Mission/ folder.
Open DJI Pilot 2, enter Mission Flight, and your plan should appear in the library.
